Vehicle Management Procedures

Procedures

Vehicle Inspection Procedure
  1. Inspection Types
    • Driver Daily Inspection
    • Pre-Load Inspection (STL)
    • Monthly Inspection
    • Bi-Annual 3rd Party Inspection
    • Annual 3rd Party Inspection
    • Spot Checks
  2. Daily Inspection
    • Driver checks:
      • HTV license and CNIC
      • Vehicle registration
      • Explosive license
      • Route permits
      • Fitness certificate
    • Complete daily inspection checklist
  3. Pre-Load Inspection
    • Maintenance Supervisor performs inspection
    • Safety-critical items marked red in checklist
    • Stop work authority for unsafe conditions
    • Block driver if regulatory requirements not met
  4. Spot Checks
    • Minimum 1 spot check per month
    • Conducted by managerial staff
    • Locations:
      • Within city
      • Outside city
      • Highways
      • Retail/customer sites
    • Critical observations may suspend driver/vehicle

Vehicle Maintenance Management
  1. Maintenance Types
    • Preventive Maintenance (PMP)
    • Corrective Maintenance
    • Breakdown Maintenance
  2. Preventive Maintenance
    • Follow OEM recommendations
    • Monthly inspection checklist
    • Annual PMP shared with operations
    • Monthly progress reporting
  3. Breakdown Maintenance
    • On-route maintenance up to 100,000 PKR
    • Use approved vendor (ATS) when possible
    • Remote areas may use local workshops
    • Inspect repairs upon return to base
  4. High Risk Jobs
    • Hot Work:
      • Check LEL percentage
      • Remove flammable materials
      • Emergency equipment in place
    • Confined Space:
      • O2 levels 20.5%-23%
      • LEL below 10%
      • No toxic gases
    • Work at Height:
      • Above 2m requires permit
      • Fall protection required
      • Barricade work area
  5. Tire Management
    • 120,000 KM mileage standard
    • Minimum tread depth:
      • 4mm front wheels
      • 3mm other positions
    • No refurbished tires allowed
    • Check manufacturing date

Vehicle Procurement Procedure
  1. New Vehicle Procurement
    1. Get client approval/requirements
    2. Obtain quotations from approved manufacturers
    3. Review costs, features and delivery timelines
    4. Consider investment policy and market conditions
    5. Issue purchase order to selected vendor
    6. Send prime mover to bowser manufacturer
    7. Conduct 3rd party inspection (Velosi/BVQI/SGS)
    8. Maintenance Manager reviews inspection report
  2. Standard Requirements
    • Comply with PATROM and OGRA SRO 2009
    • 3rd party inspection report review
    • NHA requirements fulfillment
    • Valid vehicle documentation:
      • Registration book
      • Tax receipt
      • Insurance
      • Explosive license
      • Fitness certificate
      • Route permits
  3. Vehicle Induction
    • Client approves induction after document review
    • Update operational vehicle list
    • Create new vehicle file for records
