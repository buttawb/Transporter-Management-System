Driver Recruitment Procedure
  1. All Drivers shall be recruited based on merit with minimum eligibility standards, no preference shall be given based on cast, creed, color, race, and language.

  2. The recruitment system encompasses mandatory criteria for all applicants, which must be followed in letter and spirit for the recruitment of a driver in the organization.

  3. The following criteria must be checked by the HR/HSEQ Manager before initiating the recruitment process of the driver:
    • The age of the driver should be at least 24 years and or less than 58 years at the time of recruitment.
    • Driver must have a valid HTV driving license.
    • Driver must have at least 03 (Three) years of driving experience on the type of vehicle required to be driven e.g., Articulated, Rigid, Bulk Liquid, etc.
    • Educational/Training documents of a driver (Academic & NH&MP etc.)
    • Driver must have at least 02 (two) references.
    • Driver's previous work history/character verification must be checked via email or NOC.
    • Character verification by Police.
    • Vaccination certificate against Covid-19 Virus by NADRA.

  4. HR/HSEQ Manager shall initiate a recruitment process by following the below-mentioned guidelines:
    • The HR/HSEQ Manager shall submit the job application given by the driver for recruitment and all required necessary documents mentioned above.
    • The driver shall be sent for Medical Assessment to the designated doctor/hospital.

Training Procedure
  1. Following steps to be followed for staff training:

  2. The HR/HSEQ Manager shall be responsible to prepare TNA (Training Need Analysis) based on the requirements of staff on a yearly basis or when the need arises.

  3. TNA shall be developed by keeping in view the current and future requirements that include operational, quality, environmental, health and safety, legal, and customer-related requirements.

  4. The following shall be considered when preparing TNA:
    • What the organization expects to happen and what is being happened.
    • Current and desired job performance.
    • Existing and desired competencies and skills.

  5. Training Plan shall be prepared for all identified needs.

  6. This Training Plan shall have the training needs and propose a month for training as well as training mandatory for specific departments.

  7. HSEQ Manager shall prepare Training Plan in consultation with the department so that the execution of training provision can be ensured.

  8. HSEQ Manager shall also ensure execution/provision of all external training needs.

  9. HSEQ Manager shall monitor the training program according to the Training Plan.

  10. Following training records shall be made for each training:
    • Training Attendance Record.

  11. The picture should be taken as evidence of training, and a training glimpse (ppt) shall develop.

Internal Audit Procedure

  1. HSEQ Manager shall inform each department at least 1 week before the audit.
  2. HSEQ Manager shall conduct an audit of each department, except the HSEQ department, which will be conducted by the Director of Operations, utilizing the last audit results/gaps.
  3. The auditor shall discuss the last audit action items with the relevant departments and review the documents for gap closure.
  4. After the field audit, the auditor shall conduct a closing meeting, ensuring that the findings and non-conformities are explained to the relevant staff.
  5. HSEQ Manager shall send the audit report (department-wise) to all departments for review and any required changes.
  6. Audit findings and last audit action items that were not closed shall be reported to the CEO.
  7. Audit findings shall be discussed in the next HSEQ meeting chaired by the CEO. All relevant department heads shall attend the meeting.

HSEQ Meeting Procedure

  1. HSEQ Manager shall arrange HSEQ meetings on a monthly basis.
  2. HSEQ Manager shall identify major issues regarding HSEQ within the business environment, and these shall only be discussed in HSEQ meetings and recorded in the corrective action follow-up sheet.
  3. HSEQ Manager shall record the meeting details as MOM and circulate the MOM to all concerned.
  4. HSEQ Manager shall send the meeting agenda to all concerned 3 days prior to the meeting date.
  5. HSEQ Manager, prior to sending MOM/agenda to all concerned, shall get the signature of the CEO.

Legal Compliance Procedure

  1. HSEQ Manager is responsible for preparing a list of all applicable laws.
  2. The list of laws shall be reviewed and updated on a regular basis.
  3. If there is any change, the HSEQ Manager shall update the list.
  4. BLP provides all the latest updates on changes in labor laws every two months.
  5. For environmental protection laws, check http://epasindh.gov.pk/ on a regular basis.
  6. For NH &MP laws, check http://nhmp.gov.pk/traffic_rules on a regular basis.
  7. In addition to the above sources, the following sources of information may be useful: Customer, Competitor, Industrial forums.
  8. All applicable regulatory HSEQ requirements have been entered in the Legal Compliance Register, wherein a detailed description of relevant clauses of applicable Acts and regulations has been mentioned with existing compliance status and reference of responsible authority.
  9. HSEQ supervisor shall be responsible for evaluating compliance on all applicable regulatory requirements, annually.
  10. If any non-compliance is identified regarding legal matters, the HSEQ Manager shall be intimated.
  11. The HSEQ manager then investigates the non-compliance.
  12. If the HSEQ Manager requires any resources to solve the issue, he shall request the Operation Manager.
  13. The Operation Manager shall be responsible for providing all necessary resources to resolve the matter.
  14. After the issue has been resolved, the HSEQ Manager shall ensure that the specific compliance is being monitored on a regular basis for at least 2 months.
  15. HGGC top Management is committed to ensuring strict compliance with all legal requirements relevant to the environment, occupational health, and safety Management system subscribed to HGGC operations and activities.

Risk Assessment Procedure

  1. There are five steps that HSEQ Manager shall perform in order to conduct effective hazard identification and risk assessment:

    • Identify the hazard
    • Identify the people who might be harmed and how
    • Evaluate risk and decide on precautions
    • Record the significant findings and implement them
    • Review and update as necessary

  2. All the identified hazards and their controls shall be recorded in the Risk Assessment & Control Matrix.

  3. Hazard identification can be the result of regular site walks, internal audits, customer visits, etc.

  4. Identify factors that may be contributing to the risk.

  5. For effective controls, the following control hierarchy has to be followed where possible:

    • Eliminate hazard.
    • Substitute the hazard with less hazardous control or in an acceptable limit
    • Engineering control to isolate people from the hazard.
    • Administrative control, change the way people work.
    • PPEs, protect the people with personal protective equipment as it will be the last line of defense.

MOC Procedure

Procedure:

  1. Any employee of HGGC, who wants to initiate a change or observe a change as defined below shall be responsible to inform the HSEQ Manager.

  2. HSEQ Manager then prepares MOC form.

  3. HSEQ Manager shall identify the hazard/risk inherent in the change process.

  4. The respective department Manager shall initiate the change.

  5. The respective department Manager shall be responsible to communicate the change below the ground level of his department.

  6. While preparing the MOC form, the HSEQ Manager shall give a brief description of the change.

  7. Requirements of change shall also need to mention.

  8. Final approval for implementing the change shall be given by the CEO.

  9. If any change occurred, STOP WORK should be implemented by the person who identifies/observed change.

  10. Following changes would require creating and recording a management of change form, as a minimum requirement:
    • Change in product quality e.g. Introduction of Euro5 product
    • Induction of new delivery point
    • Technological changes, e.g. introduction of new plant or processes or change in vehicle standard
    • Organizational changes, e.g. changes to key personnel staff such as a new CEO or COO
    • Legal changes, such as the introduction of new legislation applicable to the organization.
    • Changes to the type of work that the organization does.
    • Where an audit, investigation, or risk assessment suggests any change.
    • When a change is requested by a third party, such as an insurance company or client.
    • When a change is requested from enforcement.
    • When a change is required when consultation with the workforce.

Minimize the risk of injury or damage: In the event of a health, safety, quality and environment incident, where it is safe to do so, the person identifying the incident should take appropriate immediate action to minimize the risk of injury or damage (e.g. isolating the hazard, containing spills).

  2. Seek support for any injuries: In the case of an injury, depending upon the severity of the injury and the injured person’s preference, appropriate first aid or medical attention should be sought.

  3. Preserve the site of the notifiable incident: The Manager/Supervisor or person responsible for managing or controlling the workplace where a notifiable incident has occurred must ensure, as far as reasonably practicable, that the site where the incident occurred is not disturbed until the HSEQ Manager or Supervisor arrives onsite.

  4. Corrective Action: The HSEQ Manager, in consultation with the person who was involved in the incident, identifies the corrective and/or preventative actions required to prevent a recurrence of the event and develops an agreed time frame for the corrective actions to be implemented.

  5. Review & Monitoring: Once identified corrective actions have been implemented, it is the responsibility of the Manager/Supervisor to review the corrective actions to ensure their effectiveness.

  6. Investigation Plan:
    1. The following points should be considered in an incident Investigation:
      1. When and where the incident occurred
      2. How many people were involved in the incident?
      3. The direct and indirect cost involved in the incident
      4. The legal and regulatory implications of the incident
      5. Identify the key pieces of evidence (Pictures, documents, etc.)
      6. Select an initial group of individuals for interviews

    1. A team of senior management including CEO will investigate all major incidents i.e. ‘A’ Category incidents.
    2. The investigation team will comprise of the representative from HSEQ and Operations, and the report will be submitted to senior management for their review and approval of action items.
    3. Initially, the HSEQ Manager is responsible to evaluate the incident's severity based on consequence and then classify it as a 'Major' or 'Minor' incident.
    4. In-case of a Major Incident, a detailed investigation is required.
    5. HSEQ Manager shall ensure that the investigation is carried out promptly so that the evidence could not be changed/replaced/or lost.
    6. For all major incidents, the client/customer representative will be immediately notified about the incident.
    7. Minor incidents can be reported to the client/customer within 24 hours.
    8. All major or high potential incidents shall be discussed and reviewed in the quarterly safety meeting with management.
    9. Learning from the incident/REX shall be shared with drivers and with relevant staff through TBM sessions.
    10. Sharing of the incident with relevant staff shall be recorded.

  7. Recording of Incident (Major, Minor and NMPI):
    1. All incident investigation findings shall be documented.
    2. Investigation findings and recommendations for corrective actions shall be compiled in a report format.
    3. All Near Miss/potential incidents shall be documented.

Supplier Selection and Assessment Procedure

Procedure:

  1. First step: Preparations and collection of facts:
    • For the induction of a new supplier/vendor, it is mandatory to perform an induction assessment.
    • HSEQ Manager shall arrange an assessment by informing the supplier at least 1 week before the formal assessment.
    • HSEQ shall conduct assessment through assessment checklist.

  2. Second step: Short description of evaluation criteria
    • Evaluation criteria are as follows:

      • Overall Average Ratings Development Required
      4.0-5.0 Excellent Not required
      2.0-3.9 Adequate Improvement Required
      0-1.9 Unsatisfactory Alternative supplier to be considered
    • If the overall score of any new supplier/vendor comes below 2, then no work shall be given to the supplier.
    • For an approved vendor, if the assessment result score observed below 2, re-assessment shall be done after one month. No supplies from this vendor during the one-month period. Supply shall be resumed if the results found between 2-4.

  3. Third Step: Management
    • Following elements shall be incorporated in the assessment checklist:
      • Operation Management
      • Customer satisfaction
      • Quality work procedure
      • Risk management

  4. HSEQ Manager shall be responsible that every supplier/contractor is going through the assessment process at least once a year.
