Cell Phone Policy

Policy #1

Misuse of a cell phone is a safety hazard, which is why HAJI GUL GROUP OF COMPANIES has adopted a clear policy on the usage of cell phones.

Our mobile phone policy is:

  1. The use of cell phones/smartwatches/pagers/hands-free or similar electronic devices while calling, answering, sending text messages, or any other use while driving is strictly prohibited.
  2. The use of cell phones/smartwatches/pagers/hands-free or similar electronic devices while handling petroleum products or in the area where it is being handled is strictly prohibited.
  3. Usage of cell phones or similar electronic devices in the cabin by a co-driver or helper is not a violation, barring inside the terminal or any other space where cell phone usage is prohibited.
  4. All employees must intervene when they observe any violation of this policy and report the matter to the company.

Seat Belt Policy

Seat belts hold the occupants in the consequence of a sudden brake or accident, which can save lives, which is why all the employees of HAJI GUL GROUP OF COMPANIES must follow the following policy.

All HGGC and connector vehicles used for the company's work will be fitted with seat belts for all passengers.

The seat belt should have three points:

  1. Seat belts must be used on all drivers and passengers traveling for company work.
  2. All seats should have head restraints.
  3. The condition of the seat belt should be checked during vehicle inspection.

HGGC staff and contractors are also encouraged to follow this policy whether they are driving private vehicles or not on the job.

Smoking Policy

According to medical evidence, smoking is injurious to health, therefore HAJI GUL GROUP OF COMPANIES strictly follows Smoking Policy and does not allow any person to smoke in the company premises.

  1. Smoking is prohibited in all HGGC offices, Base, Depot, customer site, and while present in the vehicle.
  2. Smoking, including e-cigarettes, is prohibited in all workplaces of the company.
  3. All staff and visitors are advised to keep the company's workplaces non-smoking.
  4. If any visitors or staff still want to smoke, then smoking can only be done in the designated smoking area.
  5. Smoking inside the company's car/cabin violates the company policy.

Stop Work Policy

Haji Gul Group of Companies wants all employees to immediately stop working on any situation or condition that could potentially harm or endanger human life or affect the environment.

It is our stop work policy that:

  1. All acts/actions deemed unsafe are prohibited.
  2. All employees have the right to stop any work or work where there is a risk of HSEQ.
  3. Employees should stop work immediately and notify their immediate supervisor of the effective situation.
  4. Any unsafe work or operation can be resumed only after the Senior Supervisor has approved the resumption of the work that has been stopped.

Fatigue Policy

Fatigue is a significant risk for drivers and can be caused by constant work, improper rest, or lack of sleep. For this reason, HGGC limits the maximum driving and working hours and is allowed specific driving and working.

The driving/working limits are as follows, and if the customer's demand is better, it will prevail:

  1. A maximum of 4 hours 30 minutes (continuous) of driving after a minimum of 30 minutes of rest is mandatory. However, it is advisable to rest 15 minutes after driving every 2 hours.
  2. A maximum of 10 hours can be driven in a day.
  3. A maximum of 56 hours can be driven in a week.
  4. A maximum of 12 hours can be worked in a day.
  5. A maximum of 72 hours can be worked in a week.
  6. A minimum of 9 consecutive hours of rest is mandatory in 1 day.
  7. A minimum of 24 consecutive hours of rest in a week.

It is the responsibility of drivers to get proper rest. In case of fatigue, it is important to inform your supervisor for further guidance. Fatigue is a significant risk for drivers and can be caused by constant work, improper rest, or lack of sleep.
